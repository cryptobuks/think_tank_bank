Team

Christian Guy, MFFFFFFFFFFFFFFFFFFFFFr
Christian is Managing Director at the CSJ. Formerly Director of Policy, he has led work on a number of CSJ publications including editor of the 2011 first year report card on the Coalition Government, Building a Social Recovery?, author of the 2010 reports The Forgotten Age and the Green Paper on Criminal Justice and Addiction, and co-author of 2009's prison reform report Locked Up Potential with Jonathan Aitken.

He led the pre-general election implementation planning for CSJ criminal justice and addiction policy, and was speechwriter to former CSJ Chairman Rt Hon Iain Duncan Smith MP. Christian is Assistant Director of Jonathan Aitken's Westminster Forum. Before joining the CSJ he worked as a Community Organiser for a partnership of local authorities, police, schools and voluntary sector organisations in Surrey, and undertook a 12 month political internship in East London. He read Politics and International Relations at the University of Reading.

 

Cara Usher-Smith, Director of Stategy and Development
Cara has been running the fundraising and profile of the CSJ since September 2008. She joined the CSJ from the Stockholm Network, a pan-European free market think tank where she was Head of Media and Communications.

From 2001 to 2003, Cara worked as Iain Duncan Smith’s Constituency Secretary. Following this she worked for 2 years promoting Anglo-American relations for Dr. Liam Fox MP, when he was Shadow Health Secretary. In 2005 Cara toured the UK as part of Michael Howard’s General Election campaign team and subsequently worked in the Parliamentary Resources Unit where her research focused primarily on foreign affairs, international development and defence. Cara has a 2.1 degree in Ancient History from Bristol University and has a strong interest in addressing social breakdown and poverty in the UK.

 

Zoe Vickerman, CSJ Alliance Director
Zoe heads up the CSJ’s engagement with the voluntary sector, identifying and assessing examples of excellence among charities that are tackling disadvantage across the UK. She also leads the annual CSJ Awards programme, which recognises and rewards the best of the voluntary sector. After graduating from Oxford University in 2005, Zoe worked for an arts education charity, and was then appointed online editor of Condé Nast’s Easy Living magazine. She has worked as a journalist and editor in both the UK and the USA. In 2008 she left Condé Nast to pilot a leadership initiative working with teenage girls to raise up positive peer role models. She has been a mentor in church-based youth projects over a number of years, and frequently takes part in community-based projects working with elderly and vulnerable people.

 

Alex Burghart, Director of Policy
Alex is the Director of Policy having joined the CSJ in May 2012 from Barnardo’s. Previously he worked as a Policy Adviser in Department for Education on the Munro Review of Child Protection and at the National Children’s Bureau. From 2008 to 2010 he was an adviser to Tim Loughton MP, the Shadow Minister for Children and Young People, and was responsible for drawing up Conservative policy on adoption, child protection, special educational needs and children in care.

He is a Director of Glue, an organisation that works with young people at risk of exclusion in south London, and a Governor of Queensmill School for children with autism in Hammersmith & Fulham. He read History at Oxford and took his doctorate at King’s College, London, where he lectured and taught.

 

Robin Millar, Programmes Director
Robin provides a management consultancy service helping councils to transform some of the most deprived wards and estates in the country. This experience is captured in a CSJ publication (June 2012). He has been a local Councillor since 2003, where he holds the Families and Community portfolio and oversees development of forward planning policy. He is a former Mayor of Newmarket and was Chairman of Scrutiny for five years. In 2007 he set up a Community Interest Company to invest in the future of children and families in Newmarket. He has also held positions as Board member of sub-regional economic development body, trustee of Relate and governor of a primary school and leading Further Education College.

 

Kirsty Turnbull, Operations Manager
Kirsty is originally from Glasgow and moved to Brighton to study Cultural and Historical Studies at the University of Brighton. Prior to joining the CSJ in November 2009 she worked in administration and recruitment.

   
Dr Samantha Callan, Chairman in Residence, Family, Mental Health and Early Years
Dr Samantha Callan is a published academic and is also recognised as a research and policy expert in the field of family relationships, early years and work-life integration. She is an honorary research fellow at Edinburgh University and formerly a research consultant to major UK and international non-governmental organisations aiming to strengthen family life. In this capacity she chaired the Family Breakdown Working Group of the Social Justice Policy Commission and the Family Law Review and Early Years Commission for the Centre for Social Justice. Prior to joining the CSJ full-time she was the Family and Society Policy adviser in the Conservative Policy Unit.
   

 

Emma Johnston, Fundraising Manager
Emma achieved a distinction in her Masters in Gender and International Development at the University of Warwick in 2010 where she focused on Bangladeshi disaster management. It was also at Warwick where she gained an upper second class degree in History. Emma has voluntary experience working with asylum seekers in Coventry and was involved in a bail observation campaign where she assessed the fairness of bail trials for detained asylum seekers. Currently, she is involved in a reading assistance programme at a primary school in Westminster. She has also worked as an au pair in Brittany, France and as an administrative assistant for the Houses of Parliament. Emma joined the Fundraising department of the CSJ in January 2012.

 

Deven Ghelani, Senior Researcher
Deven worked for KPMG, a startup company and a law firm prior to joining the CSJ. He has also taught English in Japan, worked on voluntary initiatives in Tanzania and conducted research into the welfare to work sector. He is on the welfare and government spending team at the CSJ.

 

Dr James Mumford, Senior Researcher
Before joining the CSJ James was studying for a PhD in ethics at the University of Oxford, having also obtained his undergraduate and Master’s degree there. In 2003-4 he was a Henry Fellow at Yale University, studying politics and religion. Whilst a graduate student, James wrote a number of journalistic pieces on addiction, inner-city deprivation and older people in poverty.

 Lucy Maule, Policy Researcher
Lucy read History and International Relations at Exeter University where she gained a First Class Honours Degree,with particular focus on Middle Eastern politics and Political Islam. She completed a summer placement at the Centre for Social Justice in 2009 and returned as EA to the Executive Director. Lucy has worked with social justice organisations which aim to put a stop to human trafficking, and spent four months in South Thailand working with women trapped in prostitution in Phuket's red light district.

 

 

Harriet Crawford, Research Programme Manager and Policy Researcher
Harriet joined the CSJ from a business intelligence company. During her Geography degree at the University of Oxford, she conducted original research looking at the integration experiences of academic refugees in the UK. Harriet has experience working in Parliament and the national press, and has spent time volunteering for charities in both Ecuador and London.

 

Paul Langlois, Policy Researcher
Paul read Law at the University of Buckingham before working in finance. He has worked on the legacy of Breakthrough Britain: Serious Personal Debt, implementation of the CSJ drug and alcohol addictions policy, and was researcher and co-author of The Forgotten Age and Age of Opportunity.

Thomas Wardle, Policy Researcher

Thomas completed his Masters in Poverty and Development at the University of Manchester last summer. He was awarded the Brooks World Poverty Institute Postgraduate Scholarship following the achievement of an an upper second class degree in Politics and International Relations, also at Manchester. He has previously worked in market research and in political campaigning.
 

 

Rupert Oldham-Reid, Conference Manager and Researcher

 Responsible for co-ordinating the CSJ’s presence at the three main party conferences, Rupert also undertakes research in several policy areas, including employment law and charitable giving. He has a broad range of experience, working with an addiction rehabilitation centre and an MP’s office in Westminster. Rupert read History and Politics at Newcastle University, and went on to gain an LLB at the College of Law. As well as being a keen debater, he has balanced these academic pursuits serving with the Royal Naval Reserve.
